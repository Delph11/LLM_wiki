## AI Dependency in Coding (point 1)
I don't struggle with code before going to AI — I spot a doubt and go straight to AI. This is efficient but may be preventing me from building independent problem-solving muscle. See [[identity-and-impulse-control]] for the office-AI strategy angle.

## Completion Over Understanding (point 2)
I focus on finishing each module as a goal rather than genuinely understanding the topic. Speed feels productive but risks shallow knowledge. Connects to [[work-and-career]] — upskilling requires depth, not just throughput.

## Stop Overthinking — Simplicity First (Thoughts 2 pt 29)
Piyush is right: I overthink and overcomplicate. While I was worrying about document variations and edge cases, he simply said — brute force it with Claude and understand as you go. This is a pattern: I reach for the elegant solution before I understand the basic one. The fix is embarrassingly simple: start with the dumbest working approach, understand it fully, then refine. Complexity is a reward for understanding, not a starting point.

## Energy Drain from Parallel Worrying (Thoughts 2 pt 32)
I become especially tired because I focus on work AND simultaneously worry about self-improvement, habits, goals, and everything else. The Silent Monk video frames this clearly: this internal noise quietly drains energy throughout the day. The fix is not to stop caring about self-improvement — it's to give it its designated time (journalling, Sunday review per [[master-regulators]]) and then genuinely let it go during work hours. Quietly bring the brain back to the task at hand. This also helps with anxiety and tension.
