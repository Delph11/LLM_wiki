# Lust Control System

A concrete 6-step system for getting through an impulse peak without acting on it.
Source: raw/Lust Control.md

---

## The Core Insight
This is not about willpower. It's about surviving the peak. The peak always passes. Every time you pass it, file it as a win.

## Step 1 — Identify Triggers
Know what state you're in when it hits. Solitude is the main one — brain reads "alone = no risk." See [[identity-and-impulse-control]] for the morning trigger pattern and the college re-entry fear.

## Step 2 — The 10-Minute Rule
When the urge hits, don't fight it directly. Just commit to 10 minutes of doing nothing about it. The peak is time-limited. You're not saying no forever — you're saying not yet. That's easier for the brain to accept.

## Step 3 — Physical Interrupt
Exercise. Cold water on the face. Get the body involved — it breaks the mental loop faster than thinking does.

## Step 4 — Reframe the Moment
This is not something outside your control. The urge is strong but you have passed it before. If you pass the peak: that's a success, not just a neutral outcome. Add pride to the identity — "I am someone who controls this." See [[identity-and-impulse-control]] on building pride in the impulse-control identity.

## Step 5 — Distract with Small Tasks
Reading. Coding. Anything with a clear small output. The goal is not to enjoy it — just to pass the minutes. Small tasks occupy the hands and give the brain something to complete.

## Step 6 — Understand the Redirection Effect
If you control the net, the full force gets redirected elsewhere — and it will feel twice as urgent the next time it tries. Be ready for that. It's not a sign the system is failing. It's a sign it's working. The redirection is temporary.
