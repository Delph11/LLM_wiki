# Confidence and Body Language

Cataloguing the physical and verbal signs of low confidence — and tracking progress toward fixing them.
Source: raw/Confidence, talking, body language.md · raw/Thoughts.md (pts 22, 24, 27, 28, 48)

---

## The Pattern — What Shows Up in My Body
- Scared expression, jittery movements — reads as anxious before I even speak
- Look down while walking — communicates low status automatically
- Body posture stiff, absent-minded quality — feel dumb even when I know the answer
- Nervous laugh when authority figures ask questions — do not do this; turn serious immediately when a senior or authority asks something (Thoughts.md pt 64)

## The Pattern — What Happens When I Speak
- Overthink what to say, what others will think, whether I'm talking too much
- After a conversation: spiral into "oh no did I say something wrong" even when nothing went wrong
- Run out of things to say with new people
- With strangers or women I don't know — go quiet, can't give a clear answer (Thoughts.md pt 49)
- When talking to strangers (e.g. eye doctor) — felt like repeating words; need more grounded delivery (Thoughts.md pt 27)

## Brain Freeze — The Specific Problem
When Madam asked about the water: maybe joking, maybe serious — I froze. This "couldn't answer, couldn't read the situation" freeze is very common for me, especially in one-on-one conversation. The fix is not cleverness. It's lowering the stakes in my own head. Not every question is a test.

## The Banana Incident — A Case Study in Recovery
If I had said "oh okay" instead of "maine toh laayaa nahi tha" I wouldn't have been made fun of. The problem wasn't what I said — it was the defensive, explaining quality of it. The better move is always to receive and deflect lightly, not to explain.

## Case Studies — Mind Going Blank
When case studies come up, my mind goes blank. I'm stuck to a rigid method: follow rule → follow process → get output. Creativity and common sense aren't in the loop. This is a separate skill that needs direct practice. (Thoughts.md pt 48)

## One Clear Positive Signal
Basic presentation to MD sir: had a problem articulating thoughts but no fear inside. Calm and confident. That's real. Build on it — the internal state is improving even if the external expression hasn't caught up yet.

## What Happiness Actually Feels Like
At the end of a disciplined, productive day — studying hard, staying on track — there's a specific feeling: looking back with a smile, the anime after, standing on the balcony feeling proud. That's the target state. It comes from discipline, not from waiting to feel good first. (Thoughts.md pt 24)

## The Internal Identity Goal
How to create the internal identity of a confident, non-clumsy person — not a joker, not jittery. This is the core project. It's not about performance. It's about building an internal self-image that the behaviour follows. See [[identity-and-impulse-control]] for the "unabashed identity" framework.
