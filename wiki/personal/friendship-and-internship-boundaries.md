# Friendship and Internship Boundaries

How to navigate new friendships during the internship without people-pleasing, lying, or getting pulled into others' indiscipline.
Source: raw/Avoid Rlle mistake i Internship with friendship.md

---

## The Context
Making new friends: Piyush and Soham. Background lesson: mistakes made with Seetesh. The goal is not to repeat them — which means being intentional from the start rather than defaulting to whatever keeps the peace.

## The Core Problem — Saying Yes Under Pressure
Already defaulted to the old bad habit: told Piyush we'd go on tour when I will most likely say no later. This is the pattern. Say yes to avoid the pressure in the moment → lie festers → friendship goes sour when I finally say no. The pressure to agree is real, but the damage from agreeing falsely is worse than the discomfort of saying no clearly and early. See [[identity-and-impulse-control]] for the broader "saying yes on impulse" pattern and the prepared-decline goal in [[near-term-goals]].

## One Practical Advantage with Piyush
He doesn't drink. That's one variable removed from the equation. Note it and don't waste energy worrying about it.

## The Main Challenge — Boundaries Without Losing Connection
The problem isn't the friendship itself. The problem is: how do you stay engaged enough to remain a genuine friend, while not getting absorbed into an undisciplined lifestyle that costs you your own goals?

This is a balance problem, not a values problem. I don't need to judge their lifestyle. I need to design my own participation in it.

Possible approach: consistency over spontaneity. A regular, predictable cadence of showing up (one set time per week, one planned thing per month) gives the friendship a real structure without requiring constant yes-es to every impulse suggestion. See [[sequencing-and-systems]].

## The "Smart Friend" Goal
Be the smart friend — not engaged and influenced by their indiscipline, but still engaged enough to stay friends. This requires a clear internal anchor. If my identity is solid (disciplined, health-conscious, focused) then time with them is addition, not substitution. The risk is when I don't have that anchor and start moulding to whoever I'm with — see "Be like India" in [[identity-and-impulse-control]].
