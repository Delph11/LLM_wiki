# Sequencing and Systems

How to structure time and decisions so goals get done without relying on motivation.
Source: raw/Thoughts 2.md (pts 14, 21, 24, 33, 35, 36) · raw/Thoughts.md (pts 23, 51, 52)

## Evening Projects as Sequenced Blocks (point 14)
Instead of trying to do everything in the evening in an unstructured way, treat personal projects as sequenced work items — pick them up as proper projects with an order and a schedule. This turns vague "I should do X tonight" into a pipeline. See [[near-term-goals]] for what goes into the pipeline.

## IS System — Worth It for Google? (point 21)
If shifted to IS, that role is primarily system maintenance. Question: does it strengthen the CV for a Google SDE path? Needs analysis: what does Google actually look for, and does IS work contribute to that or detract from it? File a deeper analysis here once researched.

## You Cannot Balance Everything (Thoughts pt 23)
Day 2 of Kitchi tour: went everywhere, spent little time anywhere — satisfied nowhere. This happens in discipline too. Trying to do everything in parallel means doing nothing properly. The system has to make choices about what gets protected time and what gets cut. See [[near-term-goals]] — the Google SDE goal is the answer to what gets protected. See also [[trip-insights]] for other lessons from the same trip.

## Entertainment Without Time-Boxing (Thoughts pt 51)
Want to watch anime, read books, surf YouTube — but don't want to time-balance entertainment, because that defeats the purpose. The right structure: entertainment is the reward after the protected work blocks are done, not a scheduled slot to be optimised. Don't bring the project-management mindset into rest time.

## Making Decisions When Torn (Thoughts pt 52)
Often torn between two genuine goods — go with Omkar and enjoy nature vs. stay and study; anime vs. books vs. news. The paralysis is real. A decision framework for these moments: which option aligns with the North Star goal? Which one will I look back on with the satisfied feeling, not the regret? Use the end-of-day satisfaction test as a tiebreaker.

## Too Many Parallel Learning Tracks (Thoughts 2 pt 33)
Current parallel tracks: self-discipline, LinkedIn, AI/ML, algorithms, networking, impulse control, attention and confidence, LLM wiki, health and fitness — and upcoming: cooking, driving, trading. Can't keep track. This is the "you cannot balance everything" problem (Thoughts pt 23) applied to self-improvement itself. Resolution: the [[near-term-goals]] page identifies the North Star (Google SDE, 5 years). Use that as the filter. Each track either feeds the North Star directly or gets scheduled for later. Not everything needs to happen this year.

## Learning Sequence — What Comes Next (Thoughts 2 pt 35)
A clear sequence is emerging: impulse control first (already started) → confidence and attention second (already started) → problem solving and real-world problems third → initiative-taking and leadership skills fourth. This is the right order. Impulse control is foundational — it frees up cognitive and emotional bandwidth for everything else. Don't jump ahead.

## SDE Prep Will Require Trade-offs (Thoughts 2 pt 36)
When the SDE prep phase begins (likely 1 year from now), more time will be needed for it — some current self-improvement tracks will have to pause or slow down. This is fine: by that point many of these habits will be automatic, not effortful. Plan for the trade-off rather than being surprised by it. See [[near-term-goals]] for the Google SDE goal and timeline.
