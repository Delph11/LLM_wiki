## Introduction

Everything else in this wiki — impulse control, confidence, habits, networking — is downstream of two master systems. These are not tactics. They are the operating layer that all tactics run on. If these two are active and maintained, the rest becomes easier. If these two collapse, nothing else holds.

---

## System 1 — Impulse Control

**Core directive:** Control your impulses.

**The mechanism:** Delay every urge by 10 minutes. Not forever — just 10 minutes. The peak always passes. This one rule covers internet addiction, junk food, impulsive spending, lust, and impulsive speech. They are all the same problem at the root. See [[lust-control-system]] for the full 6-step system.

**The identity anchor:** *"I am a person who has control over my impulses and I do not let them rule me."*
Identity first. The behaviour follows. See [[identity-and-impulse-control]] for why pride in the identity matters more than willpower.

**The two support habits:**
- **Sleep and wake at 5am** — no matter how little sleep you got. The anchor of the day. Consistency here makes every other habit easier to maintain. A fixed wake time is a commitment to self-governance before the world has a chance to interrupt.
- **Journal daily at a fixed time, then analyse on Sunday** — daily capture prevents accumulation of unprocessed experience. Sunday analysis converts the week into learning. See [[learning-and-work-habits]] for the daily diary goal.

## System 2 — Attention & Confidence

**Core directive:** Control your attention.

**The mechanism:** Attention always outward — on the person, on the task, on what is in front of you right now. Not on how you are coming across. Not on whether you said the right thing. Not on what they might be thinking. The moment your attention turns inward mid-action, confidence collapses and absent-mindedness appears. See [[confidence-and-body-language]] for all the ways this shows up — the scared expression, the blank mind, the freeze.

**The identity anchor:** *"I am a person who is present and engaged with what is in front of me."*

**Three operating rules:**
1. **Make mistakes casually** — don't be afraid of them. Aim to notice and adjust quickly. The goal is not to avoid mistakes; it is to recover from them without drama.
2. **No hesitation** — hesitation signals doubt. Move. Adjust if wrong.
3. **No self-image monitoring mid-action** — stop tracking how you look, what they think, whether you're talking too much. That surveillance is the source of the freeze. Drop it entirely.

---

## How the Two Systems Interact

System 1 (impulse control) handles the internal pull — urges that take you away from what matters.
System 2 (attention) handles the external engagement — showing up fully to what is in front of you.

They reinforce each other: if your impulses are controlled, you are not running on a depleted system when you try to be present. If your attention is outward, you are not stuck in the inner loop that feeds impulsive behaviour.

Both are identity-level commitments, not rules. Rules require willpower to enforce. Identity requires only remembering who you are.
