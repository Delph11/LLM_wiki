# Identity and Impulse Control

Who I am building myself to be — and the impulses that work against it.
Source: raw/Thoughts 2.md (pts 5, 11, 16, 17, 19, 27, 28, 30, 31) · raw/Thoughts.md (pts 1, 5, 6, 13, 17, 18, 21, 26, 30, 31, 39, 40, 45, 47, 49, 50, 53, 55, 59, 64, 65, 67)

## Behaviour Around Seniors (point 5)
Don't go by impulse when talking, laughing, or reacting around seniors. Be intentional. This is not about being fake — it's about choosing how to show up rather than defaulting to whatever the moment triggers.

## Morning Impulse Control (point 11)
Fear that returning to college and being alone will bring the old pattern back full force. The trigger is solitude — the brain interprets it as "safe." Need to get this under control now, proactively, not wait until the environment changes. See [[learning-and-work-habits]] for the habits angle.

## Own Your Identity Unabashedly (point 16)
Example: said "I don't eat samosa due to IBS." Better framing: "I'm fitness-conscious." Don't justify your choices by referencing weakness — anchor them to identity. Trying to fit in by softening your identity will cause failure. Be unabashed.

## Build Pride in Impulse-Control Identity (point 17)
Impulse control isn't just a behaviour goal — it needs to become an identity I feel proud of. Pride creates pull; rules create friction. The goal is to associate the identity itself with positive feeling so the habit reinforces itself. See [[sequencing-and-systems]].

## Identity Contradiction — Advising Others vs. Yourself (point 19)
I tell others to enjoy life, eat what they want. But I maintain a strict identity for myself around health. Question: will playing the permissive advisor role bleed into my own behaviour and erode my standards? Watch for this. Integrity between public advice and private behaviour matters.

## Impulse to Overshare Deficiencies (point 27)
I share my limitations unprompted — e.g., mentioned I couldn't learn scooty when no one asked. This highlights weaknesses when silence would serve better. But the fix is not to become silent altogether — that creates a different confidence problem. The goal is to be honest when asked, not to volunteer what works against me.

## Confidence Style (point 28)
Open question: should I aim for Tanmay-style confidence — unabashed, steamrolling — or a quieter, more polished confidence? These are not the only two options. A third path: grounded and direct, neither loud nor shrinking. This needs more exploration. See [[work-and-career]] for the professional context.

## Being Controlled by Others — Seetesh Pattern (Thoughts pt 1)
Seetesh uses "apna matlab nahi hai toh nahi karega" and I fall into the trap every time. Need to be more assertive and shameless. This is a specific manipulation pattern worth naming — it works because I fear social rejection more than I fear being used. See [[networking-and-social-habits]] for the broader pattern.

## The 3 Control Levers — Start Basic Habit Training (Thoughts pt 5)
Basic training on the urge/impulse part: the three control levers. The starting point is not the advanced version. Start basic and build. See [[lust-control-system]] for the detailed system.

## Urge Control with Friends (Thoughts pt 6)
Difficulty saying no when friends ask to go out — fear of rejection wastes time. The issue is that rejection feels like losing the friendship. It doesn't. Actual loss of friendship comes from inconsistency and dishonesty, not from saying no once. See [[friendship-and-internship-boundaries]].

## Assertive, Less Impulsive, More Independent Among Friends (Thoughts pt 13)
This is the trifecta. Assertive: state what I need clearly. Less impulsive: don't agree to things in the moment just to make it stop. More independent: my mood and decisions shouldn't require validation from the group. These reinforce each other.

## Net Addiction — Negotiate, Don't Block (Thoughts pt 17)
Instead of full cold-turkey block, negotiate with the brain: "if I study without net for 1 hour, I get free internet time." This is more sustainable than full block, which creates rebound. The key is consistency.

## No Net Before Sleep — Replace, Don't Just Remove (Thoughts pt 18)
No net for 1 hour before sleep, then sleep. But the directive is also: don't just remove it — replace it with a healthy habit. The clue is consistency. The replacement matters as much as the removal. See [[learning-and-work-habits]] for the full sleep routine.

## Be the Decision-Maker (Thoughts pt 21)
When someone asks me — where to stop, what to do, what I want — I need to give an answer, not bounce it back. The driver incident: he asked where to stop and I asked him instead. That's abdicating. Own the decision, even if it's small. See [[confidence-and-body-language]] for the broader pattern.

## The Romantic Feeling of Satisfaction (Thoughts pt 26)
I want to chase the romantic feeling of watching nature or a beautiful anime, that quiet deep satisfaction. But it will come with discipline and task completion — not before it. The feeling is the reward for the work, not a prerequisite. Don't chase it directly. Build the conditions for it.

## Be Like India — Neutral, Uniform, Gravitational (Thoughts pt 30)
India: good relations with everybody, but a uniform stance on matters. Not like my current approach of moulding my personality to please whoever I'm talking to. If I don't agree: listen quietly and neutrally. Also like India: be good, hardworking, a problem-solver — so people gravitate to you. Friendly from a position of value, not from a position of need.

## Don't Criticise Anyone (Thoughts pt 31)
Don't criticise anybody or agree when somebody criticises another. Just listen quietly and neutrally. This is a rule, not a guideline.

## Stop Criticising Seetesh (Thoughts pt 39)
Did criticism of Seetesh today as if I have achieved something big by causing him trouble. That's not true and it's not who I want to be. Stop. Watch for this behaviour.

## Study Start Difficulty — Urge Control for Productivity (Thoughts pt 40)
When I need to start studying, I often don't feel like it and waste time on net — takes more than an hour to get started. Same pattern as impulsive net surfing: "just a little bit" snowballs. The 10-minute rule from [[lust-control-system]] applies here too. Commit to 10 minutes, not the whole session.

## Learn to Be Quiet — Don't Show Off (Thoughts pt 45)
Don't show off what I'm doing — studying CS, preparing for Google. Be quiet about your work. Let the results speak. This is a discipline of restraint that also serves the strategic goal of not making yourself a target.

## Impulse Talk — Leaking Secrets (Thoughts pt 47)
Can't keep things in my stomach — today told the Arpita case and mentioned dad's portfolio without any good reason. This impulse talk reveals information that doesn't need to be shared and creates social and professional risk. Pause before sharing anything personal or sensitive.

## Pushover Behaviour (Thoughts pt 49)
Just went to the sutta party because everyone was going. Go quiet and can't give a clear answer when with strangers or women I don't know. Both of these are pushover patterns. The underlying issue is the same: social pressure overrides my own preference. The fix starts at [[confidence-and-body-language]].

## Stop Giving Explanations (Thoughts pt 50)
Why did I give an explanation to the shopkeeper? "It's for my report." Stop giving explanations. Be confident. An explanation where none was asked for signals discomfort and invites scrutiny.

## Don't Make Decisions in a Hurry (Thoughts pt 53)
Messed up hostel room choice by deciding too fast, despite warnings. The pattern: hurry → skip thinking → regret. Before any decision with medium-to-high stakes, pause long enough to think it through. Baba was right.

## What Counts as an Impulse? (Thoughts pt 55)
Genuine question: if I use the 10-minute delay for impulse control, does that apply to checking Amazon for a due delivery, or asking ChatGPT for self-improvement advice? Or am I excusing these as "reasonable"? Am I? Answer: probably yes, some of them. The test is — do I feel the urge to do it specifically because it's easier than what I should be doing right now?

## Getting Influenced by Others' Opinions (Thoughts pt 59)
Seetesh said Sanket is bad → I treated Sanket badly. Omkar said Anish is bad → I treated Anish badly, then Omkar himself said Anish is good. I lost chances to connect with good people because I let others do my evaluating for me. In the office this has no room to recover. If the person becomes a boss — serious damage. Form my own assessments.

## Nervous Laugh in Front of Authority (Thoughts pt 64)
When a senior or authority figure asks me a question, I sometimes laugh nervously. Do not do this. Turn serious immediately. The laugh reads as disrespect or as anxiety — neither is what I want to project.

## High-Handedness with Kind People (Thoughts pt 65)
With DPM, Prayas, and similar kind types — I don't take them seriously and give them a high-handed response. When they push back I get angry. This is exactly backwards. The kind ones are often the most reliable; the high-handed response damages the relationship for no good reason.

## Position Corrupts (Thoughts pt 67)
All the CAS people were good individuals, then position changed them — they became rude, except Anish. I felt the same pull when I was leader of my case comp group. Position creates distance if you let it. Be conscious of this. It will come up in every leadership situation.

## Lion Philosophy (Thoughts 2 pt 27)
Lay lazy, rest fully — then when the opportunity appears, hunt relentlessly until you get it. Don't exhaust yourself chasing every small thing. Conserve. Strike when it counts.

## Fear Reframing — PPO Uncertainty (Thoughts 2 pt 30)
When Arpita said the environment felt different this time, I panicked about the PPO being at risk. The reframe that worked: forget the PPO and the uncertain future — focus on the certain thing, which is the learning happening right now. This internship fills the gap that was killing me in interviews (no tangible projects). That's certain value regardless of the PPO outcome. The moment I narrowed focus to the certain thing, the fear dissolved — and so did the forced, anxious quality to my office networking. Good insight: fear about outcomes poisons the present action. Anchor to what is certain and within your control.

## Never Badmouth Bengalis or Kolkata (Thoughts 2 pt 31)
Don't criticise Bengalis, Kolkata, or any regional group — even casually, even agreeing with someone else who is. Others will use it to target you in future conversations. This extends the "don't criticise anyone" rule (Thoughts pt 31) to regional identity specifically.
