# Trip Insights

Practical and mindset observations from solo and group travel.
Source: raw/Trip Insights.md · raw/Thoughts.md (pts 16, 19)

---

## Transport — Go Local First
When solo travelling, always look for the local bus or metro app of the state before anything else. Or go directly to the bus stand to learn routes and timings. This is almost always cheaper and often faster for short distances. Don't default to taxi or ride-share as the starting assumption.

## The Skill Gap — Learn to Ride
Not being able to ride a bike or drive is a real constraint when travelling independently or in a group. Gets expensive and limits options. This is a practical goal to close. See [[near-term-goals]].

## Decision-Making on the Move — Don't Break a Working Plan
Don't go impulsive mid-trip. The bus plan to Mandvi was set and working — and I broke it on impulse. When a plan is functioning, the default should be to stay with it. Changing it requires a real reason, not just a momentary feeling. See [[identity-and-impulse-control]] for the "don't decide in a hurry" principle.

## Practical — GPS for Petrol Pumps
When searching for a petrol pump on maps, don't scroll the list — zoom in on the map icons and find the closest one visually. Faster and more accurate.

## Hydration
Always carry a water bottle in hand. Stay hydrated throughout the day. Stop the water intake before the night to avoid disrupted sleep. Simple rule, easy to forget.

## Body Awareness in Public
Control involuntary body behaviour in social situations — including around unfamiliar people in enclosed spaces. Part of the broader body awareness work in [[confidence-and-body-language]].
