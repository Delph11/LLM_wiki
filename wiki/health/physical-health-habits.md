# Physical Health Habits

Practical rules for maintaining physical health while at a desk and throughout the day.

---

## Posture at the Desk

**Spine and neck:**
Sit straight so the neck doesn't lean forward — forward-neck lean leads to chronic neck pain. Keep the screen at eye level or slightly below, not looking down.

**Knees:**
- Sit with knees at 90 degrees
- Do not fold legs sharply inward toward the chair
- Get up every 1 hour for a 2-minute walk — this is non-negotiable for knee and back health

**Eyes and forehead:**
When eye strain builds and a headache is forming, looking down (lowering the screen) helps ease the pressure. Adjust screen height before the headache peaks, not after.

**The 20:20:20 extended rule:**
The standard eye rule (every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds) should be extended to the whole body: every 20 minutes, check posture, unfollow legs, roll shoulders.

---

## Eating and Medication

Taking medicine to compensate for bad eating is treating the symptom, not the cause. Stop the eating that requires the medicine. This is a cause-not-symptom problem.

---

## Sleep

- Decompress by 10pm (anime counts)
- Asleep by 11pm — strict
- No net for 1 hour before sleep; replace with a healthy wind-down habit
- Clean and prepare the bed beforehand — an itchy, unprepared bed actively disrupts sleep quality

See [[master-regulators]] — the 5am wake anchor makes the sleep schedule self-reinforcing.

---

## Chewing

Practise proper chewing consistently. Not doing so in your 20s leads to digestive problems in later life. Simple, free, easy to forget.

---

## Hydration

Carry a water bottle at all times. Stay hydrated throughout the day. Stop water intake before the night to avoid disrupted sleep.
