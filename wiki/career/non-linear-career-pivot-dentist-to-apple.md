> You can start over, even if it feels impossible.

## Introduction

Anshul Gandhi graduated from dental school in India in 2013 with a degree he knew he didn't want to use. Over the next decade, through deliberate education, relocation, and incremental role-stepping, he built a career in AI from scratch — and in January 2025 joined Apple as a Senior Machine Learning Engineer. His path is a case study in how a non-linear career pivot actually works: not a single leap, but a series of bridging moves.

## The Realisation

Gandhi recognised in his final year of dental school that dentistry wasn't his path. Rather than completing the degree and defaulting into the profession, he treated graduation as a starting line for something different. The critical move was not waiting for clarity before acting — it was beginning to build skills before the old path had officially closed.

## Building the Bridge

Gandhi's transition was enabled by two things: choosing a graduate degree that bridged his past and his future, and entering AI before it was a crowded field.

He pursued a Master of Science in **Biomedical Informatics** at UTHealth Houston — a degree he described as giving him "the technical depth I was looking for, while still feeling connected to my previous experience." The medical background wasn't discarded; it became a differentiator in health-adjacent data roles.

He also began exploring AI when, as he put it, "nobody was talking about AI." Early entry into an emerging field compresses the time needed to build credibility, because the bar for expertise rises with the field rather than ahead of you.

Alongside formal education, he built his LinkedIn network deliberately and acquired multiple certifications over the years. He is currently pursuing a one-year program at Stanford GSB.

## Career Timeline

| Period | Role | Organisation |
|--------|------|--------------|
| 2016 | Research Assistant | UTHealth (University of Texas Health Science Center) |
| 2018 | Data Science & Advanced Analytics | Health Services Advisory Group (HSAG) |
| ~2020–2023 | ML/Data roles | Dell (3+ years) |
| 2024 | Senior Generative AI ML Engineer | UnitedHealth Group |
| Jan 2025 | Senior Machine Learning Engineer | Apple |

Each step moved him closer to top-tier AI work — but none was a shortcut. The path required roughly a decade of cumulative role-building after the initial pivot decision.

## Key Takeaways

1. **The bridge degree matters** — Biomedical Informatics let Gandhi carry forward domain knowledge while acquiring technical depth. A pivot doesn't have to erase what came before.
2. **Early field entry compounds** — Joining AI before it was mainstream gave him credibility that would have taken longer to build in a saturated market.
3. **Incremental stepping, not a single leap** — Research assistant → data science → ML engineering at Dell → senior generative AI → Apple. Each role was a credential for the next.
4. **Relocation was load-bearing** — The real career trajectory began after moving to the US. Geography unlocked access to institutions, networks, and employers that were otherwise unreachable.
5. **Non-linearity is normal** — "I didn't have a linear path. I don't think a lot of people have a very pre-planned career path."

## Conclusion

Gandhi's story resists the "overnight pivot" narrative. What looks like a dramatic career change was in fact a methodical decade of bridging moves, each one building on the last. The dental degree was not wasted — it shaped his graduate specialisation and his entry into health-data roles. The lesson is not that anyone can become an Apple ML engineer; it is that a deliberate, patient sequence of credentialing steps can move someone from an entirely unrelated starting point to a top-tier destination. The prerequisite is willingness to begin before the path is fully visible.
